L7: Software Assurance

  • TOCTOU = Race Condition (fill-in-blank)
  • Code Review = "systematic investigation of code"
  • Session Mgmt = "manages ID & auth instances"
  • REST = Representational State Transfer
  • Firmware: legally protected, proprietary, voids warranty. Operating ware: meant to be changed.
  • Containerization: encrypted container separating corp/personal data (MDM)
  • COPE > POCE/BYOD (more company control)
  • NIST SP 800-163: GUIDELINE not standard. 5 guidelines for mobile app vetting.
  • Maintenance hooks: backdoor in code. Remove before production.
  • CSRF: exploits site trust of browser (opposite XSS)
  • Clickjacking defense: CHECK THE URL
  • DevSecOps = bakes security in (vs DevOps slaps on)
  • Microservices = geographically dispersed. Monolithic = centralized.
  • SOAP disadvantage: verbosity → REST
  • Input validation tools: cloud-based, open source, proprietary commercial
  • Zero-day: no fix. Isolate & segregate.
  • SDLC: 8 steps (matching). State checked every phase.
  • Embedded system: placed w/o security professional input
  • SCEP: challenge password (pre-shared secret) for authorization, NOT authentication

L8: Incident Response

6 IR Steps (MATCHING)

  • Detect → Respond → Report → Recover → Remediate → Review
  • Investigation during: Respond, Report, Recover

Key Concepts

  • 3 R's: Redundancy, Remediation, Resiliency
  • >24 hours = disaster (not incident)
  • Review IR plan every 12 months
  • Comms: out-of-band, E2E encryption (OTR), one spokesperson
  • Legal: NDAs, docs wording, assess liability
  • HR: job descriptions, removal policies
  • PR: press conferences, written responses (one person)
  • Law Enforcement: objective view, PATRIOT Act tools, rule out other causes, felonies = immediate LE
  • Senior leadership support = MOST important factor (verbal + financial)

Data Types

  • PII: understood, identified, protected (name, IDs, DOB, biometrics, financial, digital)
  • PHI: HIPAA (Kennedy-Kassebaum). OCR of HHS. Overrides state laws unless stricter.
  • SPI: credit card data. PCI DSS.
  • IP: patents, trade secrets, trademarks, copyrights. NDAs protect. No copyright govt-to-govt.

Downtime Metrics (MATCHING)

MetricDefinition
MTD/MPTDMax tolerable downtime
MTTRMean time to repair
MTBFMean time between failures (VENDOR calc)
RTORecovery time objective (MUST < MTD)
WRTWork recovery time = MTD - RTO
RPORecovery point objective (point in time)

Higher MTBF + lower MTTR = more reliable

Criticality Levels

  • Critical (min/hrs) | Urgent (24h) | Important (72h) | Normal (7d) | Nonessential (30d)

Severity & Post-Incident

  • 5 scope factors: Downtime, Data integrity, Economic, System criticality, Reverse engineering
  • Data remanence: Clearing (normal impossible), Purging/Sanitization (forensic impossible), Destruction (physical/degaussing)
  • 3 ways to read malware: Disassembly, Decompiling, Debugging (kernel=ring 0, usermode)
  • Segmentation: L3 (IP/ACLs), L2 (VLAN, private VLAN, port security)
  • Lessons learned = first doc after recovery
  • Changes → change control board (formal)
  • Evidence retention: chain of custody

  • CIA: Opposite of confidentiality = DISCLOSURE
  • GDPR 6 Lawful Purposes: consent, contractual, legal obligations, vital interests, public interest, legitimate interests
  • Data sovereignty: subject to laws where stored. 4 Qs: Where stored? Who access? Where backed up? How encrypted?
  • Data minimization: minimize to necessary. Purpose limitation: can't repurpose w/o consent.

  • DLP: prevents exfiltration. Masking: encryption, hashing, substitution, transposition.

  • Data retention: advisory role. Review periodically. One-size impossible.

L10: Risk Mitigation

  • Risk mgmt = FORMAL method (FISMA). Must be in writing.
  • BIA 4 steps: 1) Critical processes 2) Outage impacts 3) Resource requirements 4) Recovery priorities
  • Risk assessment = Risk identification (same thing). 4 goals: assets, vulns/threats, probability/impact, balance cost.
  • Qualitative: subjective (H/M/L), Delphi technique, prioritizes risks. No dollar values.
  • Quantitative: monetary/numeric, less guesswork. Complex equations.
SLE = AV × EF  |  ALE = SLE × ARO
  • Ex: AV=$20K, EF=25%→SLE=$5K, ARO=50%→ALE=$2,500
  • 4 risk methods: Avoidance, Transfer, Mitigation, Acceptance
  • Red=attackers, Blue=defenders, White=referees (MATCHING)
  • Tabletop = most cost-effective exercise
  • Compensating controls: must document w/ reason. Ex: 2 signatures, 2 keys.
  • C-suite decides critical, NOT CISO
  • Communicate risk in BUSINESS terms (not technical)
Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact / Countermeasures
  • OEM docs: anti-counterfeiting. Common Criteria (NIAP): EALs, protection profiles.
  • Fault tolerance: backup begins when primary fails. Key: no service interruption.
