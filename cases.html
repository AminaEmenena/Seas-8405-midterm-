CASE STUDIES

Healthcare (EY) + Cimpress/Vistaprint

1. Healthcare Case Study: Context

Large Medical Provider: Cancer treatment center, academic medical facility, Level 1 trauma center. Serves 2 million+ patients annually.

Why Healthcare is Challenging:

  • Electronic health records
  • Patient portals
  • Remote monitoring
  • Virtual visits
  • Cloud/web apps increase exposure

Breach Cost Impact: Average healthcare breach cost: nearly $11 million (2023). 53% increase over 3 years. Highest breach costs of ALL industries.

Detection Time: Up to 8 months to detect and resolve. 12%+ of annual cybersecurity spend just on incident response.

Complex Environment: Thousands of interconnected endpoints — computers, monitors, patient medical devices. Massive attack surface.

Healthcare organizations face the highest breach costs of all industries, with average costs reaching nearly $11 million in 2023.
2. Healthcare Decision: The Problem

Security Challenges:

  • Need 24/7 visibility across thousands of endpoints
  • Ability to detect and respond quickly
  • Proactive monitoring required
  • Limited internal resources and cybersecurity talent shortage

Business Criteria: Build internal SOC vs buy endpoint tools and manage internally vs partner with managed service provider. Each has trade-offs across cost, timeline, operational capability, long-term resilience.

Hospitals cannot have extended downtime.
3. Healthcare Decision: Options Evaluated

Build Internal SOC

  • Pros: Full control, data stays internal, deep org knowledge
  • Cons: High cost to hire/retain, 24/7 staffing expensive, long time to build capability, skills gap in market

Buy Tools Manage Internally

  • Pros: Own technology, moderate cost, faster than building SOC
  • Cons: Still need skilled analysts 24/7, alert fatigue, may miss threats outside business hours

Managed Service Provider

  • Pros: Immediate 24/7 coverage, proven experience, scalable, predictable cost, no hiring challenges
  • Cons: Less direct control, data sharing, vendor dependency, may not understand your environment
Medical facility evaluated against criteria: security effectiveness, cost, time to implement, and operational sustainability.
4. Healthcare Solution: Managed Services Partnership

Partnership Choice: Chose managed services partnership with EY.

Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR): Behavior-based tool (CrowdStrike) distinguishes malicious from benign activity. Deployed across ALL endpoints and servers.

Splunk SIEM Integration: Extensive log source integration, analytics, dashboarding. Real-time reporting dashboards give CISO instant visibility.

24/7 Monitoring and Response: EY custom detection logic and proactive threat hunting using Splunk + CrowdStrike. Around-the-clock alert triage and attack disruption.

SOAR Automation: Security Orchestration, Automation & Response. Automates routine tasks: password resets, phishing analysis, account disabling, malware removal.

Not One-Size-Fits-All: Managed services team worked shoulder-to-shoulder with client. Tailored solutions to specific organizational needs.

Talent Gap: Cybersecurity talent shortage is real. Managed services offer experienced professionals without cost of hiring large full-time team.

Automation Multiplies Effectiveness: SOAR reduced manual effort, allowed internal team to focus on strategic initiatives.

Partnership Not Abdication: Client maintains oversight and strategic direction. Provider brings expertise and operational capacity. Collaborative model.

The partnership resulted in stronger security posture, reduced response times, and freed internal team to focus on strategic initiatives.
5. Healthcare Results: Measurable Impact
  • 25,000+ devices monitored 24/7 with expert support
  • 50%+ increase in cyber incident detection and remediation
  • $80M+ estimated organizational revenue protected from cyber incidents
  • 24/7/365 continuous monitoring and response without internal hiring burden
Partnership resulted in stronger security posture, reduced response times, and freed internal team to focus on strategic initiatives.
6. Cimpress Case Study: Context

Organization Profile: $2 billion global company, mass customization of print products (Vistaprint is subsidiary). Founded 1994, Vistaprint 1999, back to Cimpress 2011.

Organizational Complexity:

  • 20+ countries
  • 16,000+ employees
  • Dozens of autonomous business units with independent technology stacks

Decentralized Model: Each subsidiary chooses own tech stack locally. No mandate from parent company. "Oversight and guidance but doesn't govern." Different from command-and-control.

Varied Maturity: Different business units have different levels of technology and security maturity.

Geographic Distribution: Businesses spread across various geographies with different internal policies and privacy regulations.

7. Cimpress Decision: The Challenge

The Core Problem: How to implement consistent security across dozens of independent business units with different tech stacks, cloud providers, maturity levels, geographic requirements?

Authority Constraint: Cimpress Security has NO mandate authority — must lead by example and persuasion, not governance.

Zero Trust as Solution: Design principle not specific technology. "Never trust, always verify, least privilege, adaptive access controls, continuous verification, decisions made close to asset."

Framework Advantage: Can be adapted to different technology stacks while maintaining security posture.

Zero Trust is perfect for highly complex distributed multi-level-maturity organizations because it is a framework rather than a defined product stack.
8. Cimpress Solution: Multi-Cloud Zero Trust

Device-First Approach: Considering health of devices connecting to network. Zero Trust endpoint protection across entire user base.

MDM and Monitoring: Mobile device management for provisioning, certificate management. Metrics to analyze, triage, monitor user behavior.

Cloud-based Infrastructure: Cimpress corporate entirely cloud-based. No corporate network. Helping subsidiaries SaaS-ify and phase out legacy over 2-3 years.

Centralized Authentication:

  • Phase 1: MFA rollout to all subsidiaries
  • Phase 2: Zero Trust authentication tool (broker) across all 16,000 employees regardless of business unit
9. Cimpress Decision: Strategic Approach

Lead by Example, Not Mandate: This was the critical strategic choice.

Phase 1: Implement at Corporate

  • Deploy Zero Trust for 8,000-9,000 users
  • Test and refine
  • Document lessons learned

Phase 2: Demonstrate Value

  • Show business units concrete benefits: improved security, better user experience, business continuity during COVID

Phase 3: Gradual Rollout to Subsidiaries

  • Recommend concepts, provide support and guidance
  • Let them adopt at own pace
Required tailored architecture that scales across Cimpress and can be applied as blueprint at each business unit.
10. Cimpress Results: Security Improvements
  • Faster Threat Detection: MTTD decreased every year, now measured in hours instead of days/weeks
  • Reduced Password Issues: Measurable decrease in password reset requests. Less burden on IT
  • Better Pen Test Results: Simulated adversaries must work harder, use more advanced tactics
  • Centralized Visibility: Better understanding of user environments where Zero Trust deployed
11. Cimpress Lessons: Decision-Making Insights

Education and Champions: Security champions program from other business areas. Developers, architects, IT/ops staff recruited to promote security. Mandate participation for corporatewide culture.

Framework Over Technology: Zero Trust is design principle not product. Decisions adhere to principles but adapt based on local choices. Flexibility for autonomous business units.

Lead by Example: Implement at parent first, show concrete benefits, recommend based on proven success not governance mandate.

Cloud Enablement: Being cloud-native made Zero Trust easier. On-premises orgs face more complexity.

Balance Autonomy and Standards: respect business unit autonomy while working toward holistic security directive.
12. Cross-Case Patterns: Common Themes

Context Drives Decisions: Healthcare = talent shortage drove managed services. Cimpress = decentralized model drove Zero Trust framework. "No universal right answer."

Phased Implementation: Both used phased approaches.

  • Healthcare: EDR → SIEM → SOAR
  • Cimpress: Corporate first → MFA → Centralized auth

"Phased reduces risk and proves value incrementally."

Measurement Matters: Both track concrete metrics.

  • Healthcare: 50% detection increase, $80M protected
  • Cimpress: MTTD in hours, pen test improvements

"Measurable outcomes justify decisions and guide refinement."

Partnership and Collaboration: Healthcare shoulder-to-shoulder with MSP. Cimpress security champions across business units. "Architecture succeeds through collaboration, not ivory tower mandates."

Success requires understanding your unique context, implementing in phases, measuring outcomes, and building partnerships across the organization.
